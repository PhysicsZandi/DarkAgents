# Critique: minimal_conformal_su2_doublet

## Verdict

Status: warning, downstream-ready for the `fopt-pta` branch.

No unresolved blocking red flags were found. The model is a consistent classically conformal dark `SU(2)_D` gauge theory with one complex scalar doublet and a single radial Coleman-Weinberg background `chi`. The validated convention for downstream use is

```text
Phi = (0, chi / sqrt(2))^T
m_WD(chi) = gD * chi / 2
m_WD = gD * vD / 2
```

This convention is compatible with the proposer/backend convention and with the real-vector mass-term normalization.

## Checks

- Gauge group and SSB: a nonzero fundamental doublet vev in `SU(2)_D` has no continuous stabilizer, so `SU(2)_D -> nothing`. All three dark gauge bosons become massive.
- Gauge boson masses: from `(D_mu Phi)^\dagger (D^mu Phi)` with `T^a = sigma^a / 2` and `Phi = (0, chi / sqrt(2))^T`, the quadratic term is `(gD**2 * chi**2 / 8) W_D^a W_D^a`. Matching to `(1 / 2) m_WD2 W_D^a W_D^a` for each real vector gives `m_WD2(chi) = gD**2 * chi**2 / 4`.
- Degrees of freedom: before SSB the scalar doublet has 4 real dof and the massless gauge fields have `3 * 2 = 6` transverse dof. After SSB, three Goldstones are eaten, giving `3 * 3 = 9` massive-vector dof plus one radial scalar dof. Total dof are conserved.
- Anomalies: no new chiral fermions are present. The scalar doublet is non-chiral and does not generate gauge or mixed anomalies.
- Lagrangian: the gauge kinetic term, scalar kinetic term, and quartic `(Phi^\dagger Phi)^2` are gauge invariant. Classical scale invariance forbids explicit scalar mass terms.
- Parameter basis: `gD` and `vD` are independent. `lambda_phi`, `beta_lambda`, `m_chi2`, `m_WD`, `gb_WD`, and `gb_scalar` are dependent. `lambda_phi` must not be scanned independently.
- Coleman-Weinberg relation: with three vectors and nine massive-vector dof, `beta_lambda = 1 / (8 * pi**2) * 9 * (gD / 2)**4`, which is positive for `gD > 0`.
- Backend compatibility: `backend/semianalytic_pipeline.py` takes `chi0`, bosonic mass ratios, bosonic dof, fermionic mass ratios, and fermionic dof. The validated mapping is `chi0 = vD`, `boson_gbs = {"W_D": gD / 2}`, `boson_dofs = {"W_D": 9}`, and no fermions. The backend adds the dependent scalar contribution internally from `beta_lambda`.

## Repairs

One minimal repair was applied because the proposal was generated by `proposer-agent`: symbolic thermal-mass placeholders `c_phi` and `c_WD` were replaced by explicit analytical coefficients:

```text
Pi_Phi = (lambda_phi / 2 + 3 * gD**2 / 16) * T**2
Pi_WD_longitudinal = 5 * gD**2 * T**2 / 6
```

These expressions match the same-model literature conventions reported by the preliminary librarian and are SymPy/Python-compatible. They are included for downstream daisy-resummation bookkeeping, with the warning that the current semianalytic backend does not directly consume these thermal masses.

## Warnings

- The exact core model has already been studied for a NANOGrav 12.5-year FOPT interpretation in arXiv:2109.11558; model-level novelty is low.
- arXiv:2109.11558 contains a convention tension between `M_ZD = gD * M / 2` and a displayed `m_D^2(phi) = g_D^2 * phi^2 / 2`. The authoritative downstream convention is the proposer/backend convention `m_WD2(chi) = gD**2 * chi**2 / 4`.
- Perturbative finite-temperature effective potentials have gauge and daisy-prescription dependence. Downstream FOPT/PTA outputs should report this as a theory-systematics caveat.
- No FOPT scan or PTA likelihood has been performed at critic stage.
